## Core Chemistry

### How Epoxy Works
Epoxy is a **two-part thermoset system**:
- **Part A (Resin):** Contains epoxide groups (reactive rings)
- **Part B (Hardener):** Contains amine hydrogen groups
- **Reaction:** Amine attacks epoxide, creating cross-linked 3D network
- **Result:** Irreversible solid that cannot be melted

### Key Metrics

| Metric | Definition | Why It Matters |
|--------|------------|----------------|
| **EEW** (Epoxide Equivalent Weight) | Weight of resin containing 1 equivalent of epoxide | Determines hardener ratio |
| **AHEW** (Amine Hydrogen Equivalent Weight) | Weight of hardener containing 1 equivalent of active hydrogen | Must match EEW |
| **PHR** (Parts per Hundred Resin) | `(AHEW ÷ EEW) × 100` | Exact mixing ratio |
| **Functionality** | Number of reactive sites per molecule | Higher = denser network = better properties |

### Mix Ratio Calculation Example
```
Resin EEW: 190
Hardener AHEW: 103

PHR = (103 ÷ 190) × 100 = 54.2

Meaning: For every 100 lbs resin, add 54.2 lbs hardener
```

## Resin Types

### Bisphenol A (DGEBA) - The Workhorse
- **EEW:** 182-192
- **Viscosity:** 11,000-15,000 cP (like warm honey)
- **Functionality:** 2.0
- **Heat Resistance:** 120-140°F
- **Cost:** ~$1.50/lb bulk
- **Pros:** Excellent adhesion, good strength, affordable
- **Cons:** High viscosity, crystallizes below 60°F, yellows with UV

### Bisphenol F (DGEBF) - Low Viscosity
- **Viscosity:** 3,000-4,500 cP (thinner)
- **Functionality:** 2.0
- **Pros:** Better flow, less crystallization, superior solvent resistance
- **Cost:** ~$2.00/lb bulk
- **Use Case:** Blend with Bis A for Tier 2/3

### Epoxy Phenol Novolac (EPN) - High Performance
- **Functionality:** 2.5-3.6
- **Heat Resistance:** 350-500°F
- **Chemical Resistance:** Excellent (acids, solvents, fuels)
- **Cost:** ~$3.00-4.00/lb bulk
- **Use Case:** Tier 3 countertops, hangar floors, chemical plants

### Resin Comparison Table

| Property | Bisphenol A | Bisphenol F | Novolac |
|----------|-------------|-------------|---------|
| Viscosity | High | Medium | Very High |
| Heat Resistance | 140°F | 140°F | 500°F |
| Chemical Resistance | Good | Very Good | Excellent |
| UV Stability | Poor | Poor | Poor |
| Cost | Low | Medium | High |
| Crystallization Risk | High | Low | Medium |

## Hardener Types

### Aliphatic Amines
- **Examples:** DETA, TETA
- **Cure Speed:** Fast
- **Cost:** Low
- **Issues:** Amine blush, yellowing, moisture sensitivity
- **Use Case:** Tier 1 primers only

### Cycloaliphatic Amines - THE CHOICE for Quality
- **Examples:** Ancamine 1618, IPDA derivatives
- **Cure Speed:** Medium
- **Cost:** Medium-High (~$4-6/lb)
- **Benefits:** UV resistant, no blush, water-clear finish
- **Use Case:** All Tier 2 and Tier 3 applications

### Polyamides
- **Examples:** Versamid 115, EPIKURE 3115
- **Cure Speed:** Slow
- **Cost:** Medium (~$3-4/lb)
- **Benefits:** Moisture tolerant, long pot life, forgiving
- **Use Case:** Industrial primers, large floor projects

### Phenalkamines
- **Source:** Cashew nutshell liquid (CNSL)
- **Cure Speed:** Fast, even in cold
- **Special Ability:** Cures on damp concrete at 0°C
- **Color:** Dark amber
- **Use Case:** Moisture barrier primers, cold weather

### Polyaspartics (Aliphatic Polyurea) - The Topcoat Revolution
- **Cure Time:** 2-4 hours return to service
- **UV Stability:** 100% - never yellows (20+ years)
- **Abrasion Resistance:** 4x epoxy
- **Cost:** High ($55-150/gal)
- **Use Case:** Tier 3 topcoat, outdoor floors, rapid-install projects

## Additives Reference

### UV Protection (CRITICAL CORRECTION)

#### HALS (Hindered Amine Light Stabilizers)
- **Product:** Tinuvin 292 (BASF)
- **Function:** Scavenges free radicals from UV exposure
- **Loading:** 1-3% by weight
- **Cost:** $40-70/kg
- **Mechanism:** Interrupts the degradation chain reaction

#### UVA (UV Absorbers)
- **Products:** Tinuvin 1130, Tinuvin 328 (BASF)
- **Function:** Absorbs UV photons, converts to harmless heat
- **Loading:** 0.5-2% by weight
- **Cost:** Similar to HALS
- **Mechanism:** Physical sunscreen for the polymer

#### THE COMBINATION RULE
**For true UV resistance, use BOTH HALS + UVA**
- HALS alone: Stops degradation but doesn't block UV
- UVA alone: Blocks UV but depletes over time
- Combined: Synergistic protection that lasts years

### Defoamers / Air Release Agents

#### BYK-A 530
- **Type:** Silicone-based polymeric defoamer
- **Function:** Destabilizes foam, helps bubbles escape
- **Loading:** 0.2-0.5% by weight
- **Cost:** $7-10/kg
- **Critical For:** Crystal-clear countertops, self-leveling floors

#### BYK-022
- **Type:** VOC-free silicone for water-based systems
- **Use Case:** Water-based epoxy emulsions

### Ceramic Microspheres

#### 3M W-410 / W-610
- **Composition:** Alkali alumino-silicate ceramic
- **Function:** 
  - Increases hardness (2H → 6H+)
  - Reduces viscosity (acts like ball bearings)
  - Improves burnish/scratch resistance
  - Maintains flow while adding solids
- **Loading:** 5-15% by weight
- **Cost:** ~$8-12/lb (but low density = high yield)
- **Critical For:** Tier 3 countertops, high-traffic floors

### Rheology Modifiers

#### Fumed Silica (Cab-O-Sil / Aerosil)
- **Function:** Thickening, anti-sag for vertical surfaces
- **Use Case:** Cove bases, crack fillers, wall coatings
- **Loading:** 1-3% by weight
- **Cost:** ~$20-25/lb

### Reactive Diluents

#### C12-C14 Aliphatic Glycidyl Ether
- **Function:** Reduces viscosity without solvents
- **Loading:** 5-15%
- **Trade-off:** Slightly reduces chemical resistance

#### Benzyl Alcohol
- **Function:** Viscosity reducer + cure accelerator
- **Loading:** 5-10%
- **Cost:** Low (~$2-3/gal)
- **Caution:** Excessive use can cause shrinkage and softening

## Application Protocols

### The "Bucket Brigade" Method (20+ Gallons)

**Personnel:** 2 technicians minimum

**Setup:**
1. Place resin and hardener drums on dollies
2. Install drum heaters (maintain 75°F)
3. Pre-mark 5-gallon pails with fill lines

**Process:**
1. **Technician 1 (Mixer):** Pours Part B into Part A, mixes 3 minutes at 300-600 RPM
2. **Technician 2 (Runner):** Takes mixed pail immediately to floor, pours in ribbons
3. **Cycle:** As T2 pours, T1 mixes next batch
4. **Critical:** Never leave mixed epoxy in bucket >5 minutes (exotherm risk)

### Ceramic Microsphere Dispersion

**Equipment:** Cowles dispersion blade (not standard propeller)

**Process:**
1. Add resin to mixing vessel
2. Start mixer at low speed
3. Slowly sift microspheres into vortex
4. Increase speed to 1500 RPM
5. Mix 10-20 minutes until fully "wet out"
6. Check for clumps (should be smooth)
7. Add hardener only when ready to apply

### Vacuum Degassing (For Clarity)

**Equipment:** 15+ gallon vacuum chamber

**Process:**
1. Mix Part A with all additives
2. Place in chamber
3. Pull vacuum to 29 inHg
4. Hold 10-15 minutes (foam will rise then collapse)
5. Release vacuum slowly
6. Package or use immediately

## Safety & Compliance

### Critical Hazards

| Hazard | Source | Prevention |
|--------|--------|------------|
| **Dermatitis/Sensitization** | Amine hardeners | Nitrile gloves, long sleeves, barrier cream |
| **Chemical Burns** | Concentrated amines | Face shield, Butyl rubber gloves |
| **Respiratory Sensitization** | Isocyanates (polyaspartic) | Full-face respirator, organic vapor cartridges |
| **Exotherm/Fire** | Large mixed batches | Never mix >5 gal at once, pour immediately |

### PPE Requirements

**Standard Epoxy:**
- Nitrile gloves (mandatory)
- Safety glasses
- Long sleeves (Tyvek preferred)

**Polyaspartic/Isocyanates:**
- Full-face respirator
- Organic vapor cartridges
- Tyvek suit
- Butyl rubber gloves

### Storage Requirements

| Material | Temperature | Special Considerations |
|----------|-------------|----------------------|
| Bisphenol A Resin | 65-80°F | Crystallizes below 60°F |
| Novolac Resin | 65-80°F | Higher viscosity - may need heating |
| Cycloaliphatic Amines | 60-80°F | Keep sealed - absorbs CO2 |
| Polyaspartics | 60-75°F | Moisture sensitive - use quickly |

### Crystallization Recovery

**Signs:** Cloudy, solid chunks in resin

**Recovery:**
1. Heat drum to 120-140°F
2. Hold until completely clear
3. Entire mass must melt (seed crystals cause re-crystallization)
4. Cool slowly to 75°F before use
