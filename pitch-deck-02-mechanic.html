Selling Mechanic Shop Floors
Auto repair, tire shops, oil change bays, body shops — high-chemical, high-abuse environments
Know Your Customer

Who's Buying

Shop owners, franchise operators, fleet managers. They want easy cleanup, professional image, and a floor that survives daily chemical abuse.

Budget Range

$4–$10/sq ft installed. Most shops are 1,500–4,000 sq ft. Total job: $6K–$40K. Push Tier 2 — best value for chemical resistance.

The Problems They Have

Oil, brake fluid, transmission fluid soak into bare concrete. Permanent stains. Slip hazards.

Hot tire pickup. Tires at 200°F+ peel cheap coatings right off. Big-box epoxy can't handle it.

Cleaning is a nightmare. Porous concrete absorbs everything. Degreasing takes hours per bay.

Customers judge by the floor. A dirty, stained shop looks unprofessional. Clean floors = trust = revenue.

How to Sell Each Tier
Tier 1 — Economy
"Basic protection — seals the concrete so oil can't soak in. Solid gray. Gets the job done if budget is tight. Good for storage areas or a shop that's getting sold."
Tier 2 — Professional (Best Value)
"This is what 90% of shops should get. UV-protected so it stays clear near bay doors. The defoamer gives you a glass-smooth surface that wipes clean with a mop. We can add ceramic for extra scratch resistance from jacks and tools."
Tier 3 — Premium (Novolac)
"This is the race shop / high-end dealership floor. Novolac resin laughs at brake fluid, transmission fluid, even battery acid. Heat rated to 350°F — hot tires won't touch it. Polyaspartic topcoat means you're back to work in 4 hours."
