Know Your Customer

Who's Buying

Homeowners wanting a luxury look without granite prices. Restaurant owners needing heat-safe, sanitary surfaces. Interior designers seeking unique aesthetics.

Budget & Margin

Charge $40–$80/sq ft installed (granite is $60–$200). Our material cost: $3–$8/sq ft. This is our highest-margin product — push it hard.

The Problems They Have

Cheap epoxy melts. Standard countertop epoxy softens at 130°F. A hot pan leaves a permanent ring.

Yellowing near windows. Without UV protection, white countertops turn amber in months. Looks terrible.

Staining. Wine, turmeric, coffee — cheap epoxy absorbs stains. Granite doesn't. We need to beat granite.

Granite costs $60–$200/sq ft. Customers want the look and durability but not the price. Epoxy can deliver both.

How to Sell Each Tier
Tier 1 — Economy (Rental Grade)
"Solid color finish that's better than laminate. Great for rental properties, laundry rooms, utility counters. Clean look, low price. Won't handle hot pans though — use trivets."
Tier 2 — Professional (Residential Standard)
"This is our marble-effect countertop. UV-protected so it won't yellow by your window. The defoamer gives it a glass-clear finish — no bubbles. We can do whites, marble veins, ocean effects. Heat safe to 180°F."
Tier 3 — Premium (Chef's Grade) ★ Push This
"This is what competes with granite. 500°F hot pan safe — put it right on the counter. Ceramic microspheres make it harder than quartz. Vacuum-degassed for crystal clarity. This is the countertop of the future."
