Selling Airport Hangar Floors
GA hangars, FBO facilities, MRO bays, corporate aviation — the premium tier of flooring
Know Your Customer

Who's Buying

Airport managers, FBO operators, corporate flight departments, MRO facility managers. High-net-worth decision makers. They expect aerospace-grade solutions. No cutting corners.

Budget Range

$3–$8/sq ft installed. Hangars are 5,000–50,000+ sq ft. Total jobs: $25K–$300K+. These clients have money — sell quality, not price. Lead with Tier 3.

The Problems They Have

Skydrol destroys everything. Phosphate-ester hydraulic fluid melts standard epoxy in hours. Only Novolac survives it.

Jet fuel / Avgas spills. JP-4, JP-8, 100LL — standard coatings absorb fuel and become slip hazards and fire risks.

FOD risk. Peeling or chipping coatings create Foreign Object Debris — can destroy a jet engine. Seamless coatings eliminate this.

Extreme wheel loads. Aircraft wheel loads of 10,000–50,000+ lbs concentrated on small tire contact patches. Standard floors crack.

How to Sell Each Tier
Tier 1 — Economy (GA / Light Use)
"Basic seal coat for small GA hangars storing Cessnas and Pipers. Keeps the dust down, makes cleanup easier. But if you're dealing with any hydraulic fluid, you need Tier 2 minimum."
Tier 2 — Professional (FBO Standard)
"This is what most FBOs use. UV-protected so it won't yellow from hangar door sunlight. Chemical-resistant basecoat with a quartz broadcast for anti-slip. Handles Avgas and light hydraulic exposure."
Tier 3 — Aerospace Grade ★ Lead With This
"Three-layer system: Phenalkamine moisture barrier, Novolac body coat that laughs at Skydrol and JP-8, and a Polyaspartic topcoat for UV armor and 4-hour return to service. This is MIL-SPEC performance. The floor outlasts the hangar."
