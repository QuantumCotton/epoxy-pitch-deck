Selling Industrial & Forklift Floors
Warehouses, distribution centers, manufacturing plants, chemical facilities — 10,000+ sq ft projects
Know Your Customer

Who's Buying

Plant managers, facility directors, operations VPs. Decision made by committee. They need OSHA compliance, durability numbers, and ROI proof. Lead time: 30–90 days.

Budget Range

$0.50–$5.00/sq ft materials. 10,000 sq ft = $25K–$80K installed. These are big checks — bring data, not feelings. Show cost-per-year, not cost-per-foot.

The Problems They Have

Forklift damage. 8,000 lb forklifts with polyurethane wheels destroy standard coatings. Tire marks, gouges, delamination.

Chemical spills. Acids, solvents, hydraulic fluid. Bare concrete absorbs everything — becomes a liability and contamination risk.

Concrete dusting. Generates airborne silica (OSHA regulated). Damages equipment. Degrades product quality. Daily cleaning costs add up.

Slip hazards. Wet concrete is an OSHA violation and lawsuit waiting to happen. Need textured, non-slip surfaces with demarcation lines.

How to Sell Each Tier
Tier 1 — Economy (Warehouse Seal)
"Seals the concrete, stops the dust, makes it cleanable. Silica-flour reinforced for compressive strength. This is your cost-effective solution for general warehousing where aesthetics don't matter."
Tier 2 — Professional (Quartz Broadcast)
"Colored quartz broadcast system — industrial-grade texture for anti-slip, color-coded safety lanes, and real impact resistance. This is what distribution centers and food plants use."
Tier 3 — Premium (Novolac + Polyaspartic)
"Novolac resin resists sulfuric acid, MEK, and hydraulic fluids. Polyaspartic topcoat means 4-hour return to service — do it over a weekend shift change. This floor outlasts the building."
