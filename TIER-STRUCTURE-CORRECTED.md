# Tier Structure - Corrected & Simplified

## Your Original Understanding vs. Reality

### What You Said:
- **Tier 1:** Off-the-shelf
- **Tier 2:** Off-the-shelf + ceramic microspheres + UV inhibitor (the "yellowing stopper") + emulsifier (for bubbles)
- **Tier 3:** High heat + emulsifier
- **Special:** Custom additives (UVA, ceramic, vacuum, ultra high heat)

### The Corrections:

#### 1. UV Protection = Two Parts, Not One
**"The thing that stops yellowing"** is actually **TWO additives**:

| Additive | Product Example | Function | Loading |
|----------|-----------------|----------|---------|
| **HALS** | Tinuvin 292 | Scavenges free radicals (stops degradation) | 1-3% |
| **UVA** | Tinuvin 1130 | Absorbs UV photons (physical sunscreen) | 0.5-2% |

**Why both?**
- HALS alone: Stops degradation but UV still penetrates
- UVA alone: Blocks UV but depletes over time
- **Combined:** Synergistic protection lasting years

**Recommendation:** Always use HALS + UVA together for Tier 2 and above.

---

#### 2. "Emulsifier" → Defoamer
The product for bubbles is a **Defoamer** or **Air Release Agent**, not an emulsifier.

| Product | Function |
|---------|----------|
| **BYK-A 530** | Destabilizes foam, helps bubbles escape |
| **BYK-022** | Water-based systems |

Emulsifiers are for mixing oil and water. Defoamers are for removing air bubbles.

**Recommendation:** Add 0.3-0.5% BYK-A 530 to all Tier 2+ formulations.

---

#### 3. High Heat Requires Novolac Resin
You can't get high heat (350-500°F) by adding things to standard epoxy. You need a different resin:

| Resin Type | Heat Resistance | Why |
|------------|-----------------|-----|
| Bisphenol A | 140°F | Functionality 2.0 |
| Bisphenol F | 140°F | Functionality 2.0 |
| **Novolac EPN** | **350-500°F** | **Functionality 2.5-3.6** |

Higher functionality = denser cross-linked network = higher heat resistance.

**Recommendation:** For Tier 3 countertops that can handle hot pans, use 60% Novolac / 40% Bisphenol A blend.

## Revised Tier Structure

### Tier 1: Economy
**"Buy it, use it, done"**

| Component | Source | Cost/Gal |
|-----------|--------|----------|
| Resin | Buy off-shelf Bisphenol A kit | ~$30-50 |
| Additives | None | - |

**Use Case:** Budget projects, rentals, primers, no UV exposure

---

### Tier 2: Professional
**"Buy off-shelf, add our secret sauce"**

**Base:** Off-the-shelf Bisphenol A or Bisphenol A/F blend kit

**Additives to Mix In:**
| Additive | Amount | Purpose |
|----------|--------|---------|
| Tinuvin 292 (HALS) | 1.5% | UV protection |
| Tinuvin 1130 (UVA) | 1% | UV protection |
| BYK-A 530 | 0.3% | Bubble release |
| Ceramic Microspheres | 5-10% | Hardness (optional) |

**Cost:** ~$35-45/gal (kit + additives)

**Use Case:** Professional garage floors, residential countertops, commercial spaces

---

### Tier 3: Premium
**"High heat, bulletproof, chef's grade"**

**This requires formulation, not just additives to a kit.**

| Component | Specification | Purpose |
|-----------|---------------|---------|
| Resin | 60% Novolac EPN / 40% Bis A | Heat resistance |
| Hardener | IPDA Cycloaliphatic | UV stability + clarity |
| Ceramic Microspheres | 10-15% | Hardness + scratch resistance |
| HALS (Tinuvin 292) | 1.5% | UV protection |
| UVA (Tinuvin 1130) | 1.5% | UV protection |
| Defoamer (BYK-A 530) | 0.5% | Bubble release |
| Process | Vacuum degassing | Crystal clarity |

**Heat Resistance:** 350-500°F (hot pans OK)

**Cost:** ~$55-65/gal (formulated)

**Use Case:** High-end kitchens, commercial kitchens, laboratories
